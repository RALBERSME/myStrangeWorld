The newspaper advertised the Baramati Marathon, which was about to take place and whose winners would receive a large prize.

Lata had hardly any time to prepare. Every day, she trained by running up the mountain with two buckets of water, dodging sharp stones and uneven terrain. Soon, she, too, was at the start of the Baramati Marathon.

Unlike the other participants, she didn't have any athlete shoes or functional clothing. She wore a saree (traditional Indian clothing) and, at her advanced age of 65, wanted to run barefoot. The organizers argued with her because this did not comply with the prescribed dress code. But Lata pleaded with them until the organizers finally relented.

The marathon began, and Lata ran as fast as she could. The hard gravel on the path tore her feet, causing them to bleed. But she kept running. During the marathon, her concentration was driven by her love for her husband and the goal of winning the necessary amount. They needed the money so badly.
