Lata Bhagwan Kare lived with her family in a small village in Maharashtra, India. Although she and her husband had worked hard all their lives, they lived in poverty. They had spent all their savings on marrying off their daughters. When the daughters left home, Lata and her husband began working as daily laborers on farms in the area. But even this salary was barely enough to survive.

Lata's husband fell ill and was diagnosed with a serious infection. Lata wanted to take her husband to the hospital, but she lacked the money for the treatment. She asked everyone she knew to support her. So she begged friends, neighbors, and relatives and collected money for further health tests. This made it possible for her husband to undergo initial medical tests.

But the doctors pointed out that further tests and medication would be necessary, which would cost more money. Lata began to despair. She had already begged everyone. Where would she get more money?

On her way home, she passed a street food vendor and received two samosas (Indian junk food) wrapped in a newspaper. Her eyes fell on an article in the newspaper, and what she read gave her new hope.
