Cliff Young was born in 1922 in Victoria, Australia.
He grew up on a sheep farm and, as a child, had to round up the cattle (2,000 sheep) on foot because the family could not afford horses. After his father's death, he and his brother took over the farm.

At the age of 61, he signed up for an ultramarathon from Westfield Sydney to Melbourne, covering a distance of 544 miles (approximately 875 kilometers). He had prepared for this race for a long time. But when he competed in 1983, dressed in potato farmer's work clothes and rubber boots, he was met with only pitying smiles. Due to his slow pace, all the other runners quickly overtook him, and he fell further and further behind. After just the first day, he was many miles behind the others. Nevertheless, he would soon prove everyone wrong.

Cliff ran for five days, 15 hours, and 4 minutes. Then he reached the finish line. He won the race by a day and a half over his competitors.
